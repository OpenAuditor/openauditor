# GDPR Breach Notification Timeline

Under GDPR (and UK GDPR), you have 72 hours from becoming aware of a breach to report it to the supervisory authority. Missing this deadline adds regulatory exposure to an already bad situation.

---

> **Note:** This is not legal advice. Consult a lawyer or your Data Protection Officer for advice on your specific situation.

---

## The 72-Hour Clock

The clock starts when you become "aware" of the breach — not when you fully understand it. This means:

- Once you have credible evidence a breach occurred, the clock starts
- You don't need to know the full scope before reporting
- You can submit an initial report and update it as you learn more

---

## What Triggers Reporting (UK GDPR)

You must report to the ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) if:
- The breach is likely to result in a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms
- Personal data was accessed, altered, destroyed, or disclosed without authorisation

You do NOT need to report if:
- The breach is unlikely to result in risk (e.g., encrypted data lost with no key accessible)
- No personal data was involved

---

## Timeline

```
Hour 0:   Breach detected or reported
Hour 1:   Confirm the breach is real
Hour 2:   Begin containment
Hour 4:   Initial scope assessment complete
Hour 24:  Internal legal review
Hour 48:  Prepare ICO notification
Hour 72:  Submit to ICO — THIS IS YOUR DEADLINE
Hour 72+: Update ICO as you learn more (acceptable)
Ongoing:  Notify affected individuals "without undue delay" (if high risk)
```

---

## What to Submit to the ICO

The ICO's online reporting tool (ico.org.uk/report-a-breach) asks for:

1. **Nature of the breach** — What happened?
2. **Categories of personal data** — What types of data? (email, password, health data, financial?)
3. **Approximate number of individuals affected**
4. **Likely consequences** — What could happen to affected individuals?
5. **Measures taken** — What have you done to address the breach?
6. **Contact details** of your DPO or nominated contact

You don't need all answers at hour 72. Tick "investigation ongoing" and update.

---

## Notifying Individuals

When to notify users:
- If the breach is likely to result in **high risk** to individuals
- "High risk" includes: passwords exposed, financial data accessed, health data, identity theft risk

How quickly:
- "Without undue delay" — generally interpreted as 72 hours from when you know *which* individuals are affected

---

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

ICO enforcement for GDPR failures:
- Up to £17.5 million or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever is higher)
- Non-reporting or late reporting is a separate violation from the breach itself

In practice, the ICO is lenient with companies that report promptly, cooperate fully, and take remediation seriously. The companies that get large fines are those that delay, minimise, or fail to cooperate.

---

## If You Miss the 72-Hour Deadline

Report immediately anyway. Explain the delay. Cooperation after the fact reduces penalties.
